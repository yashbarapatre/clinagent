# ATA Clinical Guideline: Hypothyroidism Management

## 1. Diagnostic Criteria & Reference Ranges
- **Primary Hypothyroidism**:
  - Elevated Thyroid-Stimulating Hormone (TSH) > 4.5 mIU/L.
  - Decreased Free Thyroxine (FT4) < 0.8 ng/dL.
- **Subclinical Hypothyroidism**:
  - Elevated TSH > 4.5 mIU/L with normal Free T4 (0.8 - 1.8 ng/dL).
- **Target TSH levels for therapy**:
  - Non-elderly adults: 0.5 - 2.5 mIU/L.
  - Elderly adults (> 65-70 years): 1.0 - 5.0 mIU/L (to avoid subclinical hyperthyroidism and associated risk of atrial fibrillation/osteoporosis).

## 2. Guideline-Directed Pharmacotherapy (Levothyroxine)
Levothyroxine (T4) monotherapy is the first-line treatment for hypothyroidism.
- **Weight-Based Dosing (Full Replacement)**:
  - In healthy, young, non-pregnant adults: ~1.6 mcg/kg/day (ideal body weight).
  - Common starting dose: 50 mcg, 75 mcg, or 100 mcg daily.
- **Low-Dose Initialization (Elderly or CAD)**:
  - For patients > 65 years or with established Coronary Artery Disease (CAD):
  - **Starting Dose**: 12.5 - 25 mcg PO daily.
  - Titrate slowly upward by 12.5 - 25 mcg every 6-8 weeks until TSH is within target range.
- **Administration Instructions (CRITICAL)**:
  - Must be taken on an empty stomach first thing in the morning with a full glass of water, at least 30-60 minutes before breakfast.
  - Alternatively, may be taken at bedtime at least 3-4 hours after the last meal.
  - Separate by at least 4 hours from calcium carbonate, iron supplements, antacids, or proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) which impair absorption.

## 3. Monitoring & Dose Adjustments
- Recheck TSH 6-8 weeks after starting therapy or changing the dosage.
- Once stable euthyroid state is achieved, monitor TSH annually or sooner if clinical symptoms change or weight changes significantly.
- **Myxedema Coma (Medical Emergency)**:
  - Characterized by severe hypothyroidism, altered mental status, hypothermia, bradycardia, and hypoventilation.
  - Requires IV Levothyroxine (200-400 mcg loading, then 50-100 mcg daily) + IV Hydrocortisone active treatment.

## 4. Discharge & Follow-up Criteria
- Patient is clinically stable; severe hypothyroid symptoms (e.g., profound bradycardia or altered mental status) are resolved.
- Tolerating oral intake and standard oral levothyroxine dosing.
- Clear discharge instructions outlining timing of levothyroxine administration.
- Outpatient follow-up and laboratory check (TSH) scheduled in 6-8 weeks.
