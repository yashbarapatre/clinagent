# CDC/ATS Clinical Guideline: Community-Acquired Pneumonia (CAP)

## 1. Risk Stratification (CURB-65)
Evaluate severity of illness using CURB-65 score (1 point per item):
- **C**onfusion (new onset)
- **U**rea (BUN > 19 mg/dL / 7 mmol/L)
- **R**espiratory Rate (≥ 30 breaths/min)
- **B**lood Pressure (SBP < 90 mmHg or DBP ≤ 60 mmHg)
- **65** - Age ≥ 65 years

### Score Guidance:
- **0-1**: Low risk, suitable for outpatient management.
- **2**: Moderate risk, consider short-stay inpatient admission or close outpatient follow-up.
- **3-5**: Severe risk, inpatient admission. If score is 4-5, evaluate for ICU admission.

## 2. Empiric Antibiotic Regimens (Inpatient, Non-ICU)
1. **Beta-lactam + Macrolide** (Preferred):
   - Ceftriaxone: 1-2 g IV daily + Azithromycin 500 mg IV/PO daily.
   - Ampicillin/Sulbactam: 1.5-3 g IV Q6h + Azithromycin 500 mg daily.
2. **Respiratory Fluoroquinolone** (Monotherapy alternative):
   - Levofloxacin: 750 mg IV/PO daily (requires renal adjustment for eGFR < 50 mL/min).
   - Moxifloxacin: 400 mg IV/PO daily.

## 3. Treatment Duration
- Minimum of 5 days.
- Patient should be afebrile for 48-72 hours and have no more than one CAP-associated sign of clinical instability prior to discontinuing therapy.

## 4. Discharge Criteria
- Able to ingest oral medications and food.
- Vital signs stable for at least 24 hours:
  - Temperature < 37.8 °C.
  - Heart Rate < 100 bpm.
  - Respiratory Rate < 24 breaths/min.
  - SBP ≥ 90 mmHg.
  - Oxygen saturation ≥ 90% on room air (or baseline oxygen requirement).
- Stable cognitive status.
