# ADA/WHO Clinical Guideline: Type 2 Diabetes Management

## 1. Diagnostic Criteria & Targets
- Diagnostic threshold: HbA1c ≥ 6.5% or Fasting Plasma Glucose ≥ 126 mg/dL.
- Glycemic targets:
  - Non-pregnant adults: HbA1c < 7.0% (individualized to < 8.0% for elderly patients or those with severe hypoglycemia history).
  - Pre-prandial blood glucose: 80-130 mg/dL.
  - Post-prandial blood glucose (1-2h after meal): < 180 mg/dL.

## 2. First-Line Pharmacological Therapy (Metformin)
- Metformin is the preferred initial pharmacologic agent unless contraindicated.
- **Starting Dose**: 500 mg PO daily or BID with meals.
- **Target Dose**: 1000 mg PO BID or 2000 mg XR daily.
- **Renal Adjustments (CRITICAL)**:
  - eGFR 45-59 mL/min: Full dose can be used, monitor renal function every 3-6 months.
  - eGFR 30-44 mL/min: Max dose is 1000 mg daily (or 500 mg BID). Do not initiate Metformin if eGFR is in this range.
  - eGFR < 30 mL/min: **Contraindicated**. Metformin must be discontinued immediately.

## 3. Combination Therapy (Cardiovascular Risk & CKD)
In patients with Type 2 Diabetes and established Atherosclerotic Cardiovascular Disease (ASCVD), Heart Failure, or Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD):
- **SGLT2 Inhibitor** (e.g., Empagliflozin, Dapagliflozin) is recommended to reduce cardiorenal events, independent of HbA1c baseline.
- **GLP-1 Receptor Agonist** (e.g., Semaglutide, Liraglutide) with proven cardiovascular benefit is recommended for cardiovascular protection.

## 4. Patient Education & Monitoring
- Self-monitoring of blood glucose (SMBG) for patients on insulin.
- Annual screen for diabetic nephropathy (urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio) and diabetic retinopathy (dilated eye exam).
