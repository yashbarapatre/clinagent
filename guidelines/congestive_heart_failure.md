# NIH Clinical Guideline: Congestive Heart Failure (CHF) Management

## 1. Diagnostic Criteria
- History of dyspnea on exertion, orthopnea, or peripheral edema.
- Elevated BNP (>100 pg/mL) or NT-proBNP (>300 pg/mL).
- Echocardiography: LVEF < 40% (HFrEF) or ≥ 50% (HFpEF).

## 2. Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy (GDMT)
For patients with HFrEF, the standard four pillars of therapy are recommended unless contraindicated:
1. **ARNI / ACEi / ARB**: 
   - Sacubitril/Valsartin (Entresto): Target 97/103 mg BID (starting 24/26 mg or 49/51 mg BID).
   - Lisinopril: Target 20-40 mg daily (starting 2.5-5 mg daily).
2. **Beta-Blocker (GDMT BB)**:
   - Carvedilol: Target 25-50 mg BID (starting 3.125 mg BID).
   - Metoprolol Succinate: Target 200 mg daily (starting 12.5-25 mg daily).
3. **MRA (Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonist)**:
   - Spironolactone: Target 25-50 mg daily (starting 12.5-25 mg daily, verify Potassium < 5.0 mEq/L and eGFR > 30 mL/min).
4. **SGLT2 Inhibitor**:
   - Empagliflozin: 10 mg daily.
   - Dapagliflozin: 10 mg daily.

## 3. Acute Exacerbation & Diuresis
- Loop diuretics (Furosemide) are first-line for fluid overload.
- Starting IV dose should equal or exceed 1.0 to 2.5 times the patient's daily oral maintenance dose.
- Monitor basic metabolic panel (BMP) daily during aggressive diuresis (check Potassium, Magnesium, and Creatinine).

## 4. Discharge Criteria
- Rest dyspnea resolved; no orthopnea.
- Stable renal function and electrolytes for at least 24 hours.
- Transitioned to oral loop diuretics with stable weight for 24 hours.
- Clear outpatient follow-up scheduled (within 7-14 days).
